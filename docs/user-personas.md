# User Personas — ismybabyalright.com

These personas represent the range of people who might land on a site called
"ismybabyalright.com." No solutions prescribed yet — just who they are, what
they feel, and what brought them here.

---

## Persona 1: "Nadia" — The 2am Googler

**Age:** 28 | **Location:** Austin, TX | **Baby:** 4-month-old son (first child)
**Occupation:** Marketing coordinator (on maternity leave)
**Tech comfort:** High — iPhone, Instagram, Reddit

### Story
Nadia had a healthy pregnancy. But the moment she brought her son home, a low hum of worry switched on and never turned off. She finds herself Googling things during night feedings — "4 month old not rolling over," "is green poop normal," "baby won't make eye contact." Her pediatrician is great but appointments are every few months, and she doesn't want to call the nurse line for things that might be "stupid questions." She feels embarrassed about how much she worries.

### What triggers her to search
- Baby does something she hasn't seen before (or stops doing something)
- A friend's baby the same age is doing something hers isn't
- She reads a parenting post on Instagram that mentions a milestone she didn't know about
- Something looks "off" physically — a rash, weird poop color, a soft spot that seems too big

### What she's feeling
- Anxiety mixed with guilt about being anxious
- Isolation — up alone at 2am, partner asleep
- Information overload — she's read too much and can't filter signal from noise
- Imposter syndrome as a mom — "shouldn't I just know this?"

### Current coping
- Googles obsessively, reads 10+ results, ends up more confused
- Texts her mom friend group but gets conflicting anecdotal advice
- Screenshots parenting infographics from Instagram
- Saves WebMD/Healthline articles she never re-reads

### Frustrations
- Google results are terrifying medical papers or dead forum threads from 2011
- Every AI answer ends with "consult your pediatrician"
- Different sources list different milestone timelines
- She doesn't know what questions to even ask at the next appointment
